About

Give Models Physics

Named for Mnemosyne — the Greek Titaness of memory and mother of the Muses — our institute builds tools and theory for AI systems that fail less by being constrained more.

⬡

Geometric AI Theory

Typed embeddings as geometric priors. Frozen type structure introduces constraint into model outputs — making certain failure modes geometrically impossible, not just statistically unlikely. External validation: Bochkov (TMLR 2025).

✓

Formal Verification

Training data that is verified by SymPy and Lean before it enters a model. The ChatDB proof engine targets the IMO corpus — generating millions of typed, verified reward signals that mean what they say.

♟

Empirical Benchmarking

Chess as a measurement instrument. The Oracle Trust Calibration Framework pits frontier LLMs against Stockfish at controlled ELO ratings to observe, categorize, and publish cognitive failure modes at scale.

⚙

Engineering Tools

ProveCalc brings the constraint-as-information thesis into daily engineering practice: verified calculations, dimensional analysis, PE-stamp-ready audit trails, and perpetual desktop licensing.

Findings

Empirical Results

What we've actually found. Not theory — observed, documented, reproducible.

01

Verification ≠ Exploration

19 of 19 locally verified proof steps on IMO 2025 Problem 3 still produced the wrong final answer (c=3 instead of c=4). Local verification is necessary but not sufficient. The obligation DAG architecture is the fix.

ChatDB · IMO 2025 P3
02

The Gemini Paradox

Same model, same day, different games: tactical brilliance followed by complete endgame collapse. Within-session performance variance is itself a measurable and publishable finding about model reliability.

OTCF · LLM Chess Arena
03

Forced-Move Blindness

LLMs consistently fail to find the only legal response when in check — a class of failure distinct from tactical error. Nemotron exhibits this pattern with high reproducibility across sessions.

OTCF · Nemotron Pattern
04

Representational Interference

Frozen embeddings outperform trainable embeddings (Bochkov, TMLR 2025). Our explanation: trainable embeddings allow representational interference that destroys geometric type structure. Freezing is a constraint that preserves information.

ANLU · Bochkov (TMLR 2025)
05

Context Saturation Degradation

LLM chess performance degrades as prompt length increases — even when all added context is relevant. More information past a threshold actively hurts performance. Measured across P0–P6 prompt density spectrum.

OTCF · Prompt Density Study
06

Endgame Collapse

Tactical strength drops sharply when piece count falls below a threshold. LLMs that perform at 1400+ ELO in complex middlegames drop to sub-800 in simplified endgames — a phase-transition failure mode.

OTCF · Phase Analysis
